ARE LOCATIONS GENDER BIASED?

The demand for female art is less in certain countries. It is believed that auction houses may avoid selling collections containing a large number of female works. After all, everything revolves around profit in this world. The question that remains is whether there a significant difference between the female and male data.



USED DATA: the relative difference between the hammer price and the estimated price per location.
Firstly, the difference between hammer price and the average estimated price for each piece of art was calculated. Then it was calculated how much this difference was in percentage of the hammer price, which resulted in a relative difference. Then, the median of all works sold in a specific location was calculated because the number of artworks in the location are not the same.

From the following graph it can be concluded that Spain, Austria, United States, Germany and Belgium are the most emancipatory countries. Their estimates of female and male art have largely the same price results. Countries that overestimate the estimated price are not yet ready for female art: United Kingdom, Italy, Ireland, France, Korea, Monaco, Sweden, United Arab Emirates, Canada, Qatar and New Zealand. Countries that underestimate female artworks: South Africa, Israel, Taiwan, Norway, Hong Kong, Turkey, China, Australia, Japan, Switzerland, Denmark, Netherlands, South Korea, Czech Republic, Singapore and Romania.

The world map shows the distribution of these three kind of price settings.

From the same data a case study was set up which can be seen in the next visualization panel with the top 4 locations and their possible gender biased appreciation of artwork.

It can be concluded that Spain, Austria, United States, Germany and Belgium are the most emancipatory countries. Their estimates of female and male art results largely in the same price settings. Concerning the top four countries in the contemporary auction world (USA, UK, China and France), there is no unambiguous outcome noticeable. The US correctly estimated all its hammered works regardless of gender. Surprisingly, the UK scores differently. In the UK, the female art is overrated just as in France. In China female art is overestimated. It shows that the buyers public is ready to bid, but the auction houses there are still a bit hesitant regarding the setting of the estimated price.
