GENERAL OVERVIEW


Is gender-biased appreciation of artwork geographical determined? And which auction houses are the most popular?

USED DATA: This part shows the number of records of the female artists compared to that of their male colleagues linked to auction houses and location. The data was retrieved from the database Art Price of the top 100 contemporary artists (including 12 women).

As a general result, it was concluded that the number of artworks sold at auction houses has more or less the same gender spreading. The hypothesis that the duopoly Sotheby’s and Christie’s and the countries United States and United Kingdom take the lead turned out to be correct: Sotheby’s and Christie’s are for both genders the top auction houses; and the United States and the United Kingdom the top locations.

From hovering over and/or clicking on the graph below you can see that the theory of the above paragraph can be confirmed: the center (USA, UK and China, Japan and Hong Kong) is more or less evenly distributed regardless of gender. In turn, the semi-periphery and the periphery (not USA and UK) show more distribution between male and female artists.

Another visualization down below shows how many records there are linked to a specific auction house. The top 100 of Contemporary artist appears to follow the general pattern of both male and female artists. The three top auction companies for both genders are Christies, Sotheby's and Phillips.

From interacting with the graph it can be seen that the number of records of the auction houses in the top 100 of contemporary artists for female as male data are not that different. As a percentage, Christie's sells 21.34% of the total lots for woman artists. For men it counts to a percentage of 15.00% of their total lots. Sotheby's: 17,50% for female artists, 13.60% for male artists. Phillips: for female artists 5.91% and for male artists 4.29%.


CONCLUSION

It can be concluded that the number of records of the auction houses in the top 100 of contemporary artists for female and male data are not that different. The results indicate that Sotheby’s and Christie’s are for both genders the top auction houses; and the United States and the United Kingdom the top locations. The gender division of the amount of artworks in the auction world is more or less divided the same concerning the center (USA, UK and China, Japan and Hong Kong). When looking at the semi-periphery and the periphery there is a more uneven distribution.
